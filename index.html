The Half Moon Society is a community led initiative to get people to donate blood to help sickle cell disease in communities in London. Building a society for the community of adults that are already going through the pain of it, those who have never heard of the disease and the children who need the support from their peers, teachers and loved ones.
Sickle cell disease is a blood disorder which affects your heamoglobin, the molecule that delievers your oxygen to around your body. When you have sickle cell disease the body struggles to get blood around the body which leads to severe pain and anemia.People with sickle cell are more at risk of stroke and bone damage. Prevenetng and treating sickle cell crisis (painful episodes) are managed through blood transfusions and painkillers.

In England, 1 in every 2000 live births a child is born with sickle cell.
